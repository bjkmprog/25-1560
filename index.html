Origins

Betrayal by Nintendo

PlayStation began as a Nintendo partnership project that failed, leading Sony to create their own console. Started in the early 1990s when Sony and Nintendo planned a CD-ROM add-on for the SNES, but Nintendo backed out, prompting Sony to enter the gaming market independently.

PlayStation 1 (1994)

The revolution begins! First major console to use CDs instead of cartridges, making games cheaper and allowing full-motion video. Introduced 3D gaming to the masses with iconic titles like Crash Bandicoot and Final Fantasy VII. Sold over 100 million units.The PlayStation 1 revolutionized gaming with its CD-ROM technology, replacing cartridges with discs that could hold 650MB of data - over ten times more than typical cartridges. It featured a 33.9MHz R3000 processor and dedicated 3D graphics hardware capable of rendering 360,000 polygons per second. This allowed for full-motion video, CD-quality audio, and expansive 3D worlds that were impossible on cartridge-based systems.

PlayStation 2 (2000)

The king of consoles! Best-selling game console of all time (155+ million units). Built-in DVD player made it a home entertainment hub. Featured groundbreaking games like Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas and introduced online gaming with the Network Adapter.The PS2's groundbreaking "Emotion Engine" processor ran at 300MHz and could push 75 million polygons per second, making it the most powerful console of its generation. Its built-in DVD player was revolutionary, transforming the console into a complete home entertainment system. With 32MB of RDRAM and full backward compatibility with PS1 games, it offered both power and value that dominated the market.

Playstation 3 (2006)

The powerhouse era! Featured the complex Cell processor and built-in Blu-ray player. Launched PlayStation Network for online gaming and digital purchases. Struggled initially due to high price but recovered with strong exclusives like The Last of Us.The PS3 featured the complex Cell Broadband Engine, a 3.2GHz processor with seven synergistic processing elements that delivered supercomputer-like performance. Its integrated Blu-ray drive not only provided 25-50GB of storage for games but also helped Sony win the high-definition format war against HD-DVD. With 256MB of XDR main memory and 256MB of GDDR3 video memory, it was a true multimedia powerhouse.

PlayStation 4 (2013)

Return to glory! Focused on games-first approach after PS3's rocky start. Dominated the generation with incredible exclusives like God of War and Spider-Man. Introduced Share button for social gaming and built a massive online community.The PS4 moved to familiar x86 architecture with an 8-core AMD Jaguar processor and 8GB of unified GDDR5 memory, making development easier and more efficient. Its AMD Radeon graphics processor delivered 1.84 teraflops of performance, focusing on 1080p gaming and social features. The innovative Share button allowed instant streaming and recording, while the upgraded hard drive supported massive game installations.

Playstation 5 (2020)

Next-gen evolution! Lightning-fast SSD eliminates loading times. DualSense controller with haptic feedback and adaptive triggers. Supports 4K/120fps gaming and ray tracing. Continues PlayStation's legacy of premium gaming experiences.The PS5's custom AMD Zen 2 processor and RDNA 2 graphics architecture deliver 10.28 teraflops of power, supporting 4K gaming at 120fps with ray tracing. Its ultra-fast 825GB SSD eliminates loading times entirely, streaming data at 5.5GB/s. The revolutionary DualSense controller features haptic feedback and adaptive triggers that provide physical sensations corresponding to in-game actions.
